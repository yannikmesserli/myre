Inpainting of depth map

From the 20th February
2012 to the 16th June 2012
Prof. Frossard
Thomas Maugey

A commonly adopted data representation for multi-view video transmission is based on texture and depth description. In other words, some reference views transmit a color image and its corresponding depth image. An important issue in this technic is the filling of the missing regions in the projected image due to occlusions in the scene. While the existing algorithms mainly consider the inpainting of the projected texture images, few techniques hansectione the problem of depth image filling.

The task of my teammate and I was to design a depth inpainting technic. We compared a classical approach through modifications of the M. Bertalmio and A. Criminisi approach with a designed one specific to the depth map problem. The later has been designed by segmenting the image into regions supoosed to be described by simple plane equation. Then, the coefficients of the plane are recovered by the statistics of the region and the region is inpainted.

Check the matlab code on Github.

The aim of this project was to characterize a digital camera by experimentally finding its gamma correction and a relationship between the CIE-XYZ color space. Master project

From the 19th September
2011 to the 16th January 2012
Mark Pauly
Sofien Bouaziz

Constrained Local means (CLM) have been proposed with a lot of promise to solve the problem of de- formable model fitting. It predicts landmarks positions through two models: a point distribution model (PDM) that ensures a valid shape, and some templates that describe a local image response of a landmark. The first model constraints the second. However CLM has given poor results under some difficult light con- ditions or under strong noise.

I proposed to Prof. Pauly to improve the templates by using the Discrete Bochner Laplacian instead of the color texture. It is a derivation for a discrete Laplacian introduced by Robin Forman through the so-called ”Bochner Method”. The 2D case is derivated in Eli and Emil’s paper on which I worked during my stay at the technion (See below). My task was to implement it and to ran a lot tests to compare technics.

Project at the Technion

From the 24th July
to the 16th September 2011
Eli Appleboim
Emil Saucan

The subject of my internship was topics related to the Discrete Bochner Laplacian. It is a derivation for a discrete Laplacian introduced by Robin Forman through the so-called ”Bochner Method”. The 2D case is derivated in Eli and Emil’s paper.

The first task was to compare the Voxel’s bochner laplacian and the image’s one. It has been done by computing for different weight values for beta, the smooth factor of the Bochner formulas.

I proposed also to use the Bochner Laplacian to improve of the Non-local Means algorithm. The idea was to denoise pixels values with same curvature score so that the denoising process do not smooth the image. Read more.Coming soon

I also used the curvature to reconstruct 3D object from 2D images sets.

There is many reason we want to compute the bochner laplacian on 3D object. Combinatorial Bochner laplacian along a triangulation has also been developped and tested. Finally I proposed different weights computation.

Bachelor project

From the 20th Septembre
2010 to the 8th January 2011
Prof. Vandergheynst
Emmanuel D'Angelo

Non-Local Means have become very popular in image denoising. Its goal is to remove a noise from an image. Prof. Buades introduced a clever method of averaging by finding similar pixels in a portion of the image. This similarity is evaluated by comparing a whole patches around pixels, not only their value, and comparing the euclidean distance.

Conceptually simple, this algorithm gives really good but time consumption is however a major drawback. The goal of my semester project was to implement NLM with a specific data structure in order to accelerate the processus. The «spectral hashing» of Professor Yair Weiss has been used to compute a score independently of the pixel position and I have been able to store it to avoid redundancy computations. Implemented with openCV, the results was surprisingly fast. Moreover the architecture is easily used in concurrency to speed up the process.
